## Design Context

### Users
Restaurant workers (kitchen staff, servers, managers) at Everest Sushi & Fusion AS. Used primarily on wall-mounted tablets in a kitchen environment and on desktop by managers. Operating under time pressure — a line cook should be able to log a temperature reading in 10 seconds without reading anything carefully. Users range from tech-comfortable managers to staff who may not use apps often. Interface must communicate status (OK / warning / critical) at an absolute glance.

### Brand Personality
Simple, practical, effective. This is a compliance tool, not a consumer app. It should feel like an official system — the kind you trust because it's clearly built for a serious purpose, not designed to delight or impress. Think of the physical object: a laminated A4 inspection checklist on a Norwegian kitchen wall, or a Skatteetaten government portal login screen. Authority through clarity.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Norwegian Institutional Minimalism.** References: Skatteetaten.no, Brønnøysundregistrene.no — not flashy, not consumer-app, not startup SaaS. Clean surfaces, strong typographic hierarchy, functional status colors, no decorative elements whatsoever. Status colors (green/amber/red) are the only real visual ornamentation — they must remain strong and readable. Light mode throughout (restaurant lighting; dark mode creates glare and scanning difficulty). Dark login screen is an intentional contrast moment — "you are entering an official system" — but refined vs. generic SaaS gradient.

Anti-references: Apple homepage aesthetic (too consumer, too polished), generic SaaS dashboard (purple gradients, sparklines, hero metrics, etc.), food delivery apps (too playful, too consumer-friendly feel).

**Font:** Albert Sans — clean, geometric, Nordic character. Replaces Inter. Weights 400/500/600/700.

**Colors:** OKLCH-based for perceptual uniformity. All neutral surfaces tinted very subtly toward the primary green hue (institutional cohesion). Primary green = food safety. Status colors are the decoration — everything else is subordinate.

**Shape:** Slightly more angular corners than typical SaaS (r: 8px, r-lg: 10px). More institutional feel. Shadows are very subtle — not the big diffuse clouds of startup design.

### Design Principles
1. **Status at a glance** — critical information (temperatures, deviations, compliance %) must be readable in 1 second from arm's reach on a tablet
2. **No decoration for decoration's sake** — if a visual element doesn't communicate something, it doesn't exist
3. **Institutional trust** — the interface should feel like it belongs in a compliance context, not a consumer app
4. **Touch-first at all times** — 44px minimum tap targets, generous spacing between interactive elements
5. **Typography does the work** — size and weight contrast carry hierarchy; color is only for status states, not aesthetic
